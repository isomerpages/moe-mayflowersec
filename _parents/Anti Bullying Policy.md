<h2><strong>Anti-Bullying Policy: Our Commitment to a Safe Community</strong></h2>
<p>At Mayflower Secondary School, every student has the right to learn in
a safe, enabling, and respectful environment. We are committed to fostering
a gracious school community where every individual is valued and protected.
We want to nurture students who are kind, supportive and respectful.</p>
<h3><strong>Our Stance: Zero Tolerance Towards Bullying</strong></h3>
<p>Mayflower Secondary School maintains a zero tolerance policy towards bullying.
We take a firm stance against all forms of hurtful behaviour. We do not
distinguish between "minor" or "major" incidents when it comes to the dignity
of our students; every report is taken seriously. We provide support for
students affected by bullying, corrective guidance to those whose behaviour
has hurt others and restoration to all parties involved.</p>
<h3><strong>Defining Bullying</strong></h3>
<p>Bullying is the wilful conscious desire to hurt, threaten or frighten
someone else. It can be a one-off incident or a persistent, intentional
act. This includes, but is not limited to:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Verbal:</strong> Insensitive comments, name-calling, or spreading
rumors.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Physical:</strong> Any form of physical violence or intimidation.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Social/Psychological:</strong> Exclusion, gestures, or hostile
body language.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Cyberbullying:</strong> Harassment via social media, text, or digital
platforms.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>Our Proactive Approach</strong></h3>
<p>We do not just react to bullying; we work to prevent it through:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Character and Citizenship Education (CCE):</strong> Equips students
to build respectful relationships, recognise and stand up against bullying
both online and offline and develop the confidence to be positive influences
who support their peers.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Peer Support:</strong> A trained network of students who act as
the "eyes and ears" of the school, offering a first line of support to
their peers and positively influence them.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Communication:</strong> Regular briefings for both students and
staff to ensure everyone recognises the signs of bullying and actions to
take should it occur.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>We adopt a positive and proactive discipline approach to develop our students to be kind, caring, assertive and to seek help when targeted.</strong>
</p>
<h3><strong>Reporting and Intervention</strong></h3>
<p>We provide accessible and safe reporting channels. Students can report
incidents to any teacher, Year Head, Student Management Team (SMT) member,
School Counsellor, School Leader or use our dedicated internal feedback
platforms.</p>
<h3><strong>Our Response Process:</strong></h3>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Safety Plans: </strong>Immediate measures to ensure the victim
feels safe and supported at school.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Communication: </strong>Engage parents promptly upon becoming
aware of an incident. We will reach out to both parties to acknowledge
the situation, lay out our follow-up actions and provide regular updates
along the process.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Investigation: </strong>Prompt and thorough fact-finding to understand
the situation.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Consequences: </strong>Consequences<strong> </strong>meted out
aligned with the school’s code of conduct.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Restoration: </strong>We believe in <strong>Restorative Practice</strong>.
We seek to facilitate the restoration of relationships through guided mediation
and counselling to heal the community.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>Partnership with Parents</strong></h3>
<p>We believe that a safe school environment is a shared responsibility.
We hope parents would partner the school in this effort by:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Monitoring their child’s well-being and relationship with their peers.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Monitoring their online activity and guiding them to refrain from liking,
sharing or commenting on hurtful posts.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Communicating openly with the school if they suspect bullying is occurring,
whether of their child or of their peers.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Reinforcing the values of respect and empathy at home.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Supporting and guiding their child to take on upstanding behaviours (i.e.
provide a listening ear, show empathy, defend the victim and report the
incident to a teacher).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Encouraging their child to look out for friends in need and provide support
to them.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h4><strong>Our Promise:</strong></h4>
<p>All students learn and grow best in a caring and enabling Mayflower Community.
We are committed to acting swiftly and fairly to ensure our school remains
a safe and supportive environment for all students.</p>
