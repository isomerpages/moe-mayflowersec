<h4><strong>What Learning Looks Like in Our Classrooms</strong></h4>
<p>Our students are active participants in their learning, not passive recipients
of information.</p>
<p><strong>In a Humanities classroom, you will often see students:</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Discussing real-world issues</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Interpreting data and case studies</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Evaluating historical sources</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Giving and receiving peer feedback</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Reflecting on their own thinking</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Using digital platforms to support inquiry</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Our lessons intentionally develop:</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Critical and adaptive thinking</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Communication and collaboration skills</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Independent learning habits</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Confidence in expressing ideas using evidence</p>
</li>
</ul>
