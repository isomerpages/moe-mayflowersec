<h2>Character and Citizenship Education</h2>
<p>At Mayflower Secondary School, our CCE programme shapes students into
well-rounded individuals prepared to contribute to society. Through lessons,
practical experiences, and learning that connects different subjects, we
focus on developing four key areas in every student.</p>
<h3>Our CCE Goals</h3>
<p><strong>Good Character</strong> forms the foundation of our programme,
where we guide students to develop a sound moral compass and strong sense
of right and wrong. Students learn to think critically and ethically, take
responsibility for their choices, show care for others, and strive for
excellence in all they do.</p>
<p>Our character development follows a progressive values framework across
the secondary years.</p>

<p><strong>Resilience and Social-Emotional Well-being</strong> is cultivated
through helping students develop a balanced sense of self and the ability
to form healthy relationships. Our programme builds resilience to face
life's challenges, supports students in finding meaning and purpose, and
nurtures gratitude and appreciation for life's experiences.</p>
<p><strong>Active Citizenship</strong> develops through fostering a strong
national identity rooted in genuine belonging to Singapore. Students gain
hope in themselves and the future whilst developing awareness of Singapore's
unique vulnerabilities and constraints.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Future Readiness</strong> ensures our students develop a clear
sense of purpose in life alongside the essential dispositions of adaptability
and lifelong learning. This preparation enables them to navigate education
and career pathways purposefully and confidently take on future challenges,
including the evolving world of work.</p>

<h3>How We Achieve These Goals</h3>
<h4>CCE Lesson</h4>
<p>Our structured CCE lessons provide explicit teaching of school values
and social-emotional competencies to address students' holistic developmental
needs. Students learn to understand and regulate their emotions, manage
relationships effectively, and develop their sense of purpose in life.
These lessons equip students with knowledge and skills to better understand
real-world contexts through discussions on contemporary issues, enabling
them to grasp current community and global realities whilst reflecting
on their national identity and developing civic consciousness.</p>
<h4>Key Student Development Experiences</h4>
<p>We provide rich experiential learning opportunities that contribute to
holistic development across physical, aesthetic, intellectual, moral and
social domains. These include Co-Curricular Activities (CCAs), cohort learning
journeys, Education and Career Guidance experiences, National Education
Commemorative Days, NRIC Presentation Ceremonies, Outdoor Adventure Learning
cohort camps, Student Leadership Development programmes, and Values-in-Action
projects.</p>
<h4>Cross-Curricular Integration</h4>
<p>Our CCE objectives are reinforced through other subjects including Social
Studies, History, Music, and Art, which provide content knowledge and opportunities
for exploring national identity, contemporary issues, and Singapore's constraints
and vulnerabilities. Physical Education contributes by teaching sportsmanship
and encouraging students to take responsibility for maintaining a healthy
lifestyle.</p>
<p>Through this comprehensive and integrated approach, Mayflower's CCE programme
prepares our students not just for academic success, but for life as engaged,
responsible, and contributing citizens of Singapore and the world.</p>
